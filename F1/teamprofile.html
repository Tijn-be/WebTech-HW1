History

Formula 1 became an official world championship in 1950 and has grown into the highest-profile form of international single-seater racing. Across its history, the series has combined technical innovation, global travel, and intense competition between drivers and constructors.

League Structure

Each Formula 1 season is built around teams entering two cars, a calendar of Grands Prix, and championship points awarded based on race results. Success depends not only on the drivers, but also on engineering, strategy, reliability, pit work, and long-term car development.

Featured Circuits

Formula 1 is defined by a mix of classic venues and modern host circuits. Tracks such as Silverstone, Monza, Suzuka, Spa-Francorchamps, and Monaco are widely associated with the sport's history, atmosphere, and competitive identity.
